---

## The hook

> **Test any product, pricing, or CX decision on thousands of synthetic customers — before you spend a dollar or wait six weeks for a real panel.**

---

## The problem (EXL's clients feel this every quarter)

- Real customer research is **slow** (weeks) and **expensive** (panels, incentives, ops).
- Insurers, banks, and health plans launch pricing changes, new products, and retention offers with **thin upfront signal** — then learn from the market, expensively.
- Hard-to-reach micro-segments (niche risk pools, low-tenure cohorts) are nearly impossible to survey at a usable sample size.

## The idea

A **decision twin** that turns your customer data into a pool of AI personas, then simulates how each segment reacts to a proposed decision — in minutes, not weeks.

1. **Personas** — generated from (or imported from) your real customer data.
2. **Audiences** — segment into the exact cohort the decision targets.
3. **Play** — define the product/pricing/CX change and its hypothesis.
4. **Simulate** — survey mode (intent, sentiment, concerns) or focus-group debate mode.
5. **Verdict** — *ship / hold / redesign*, with segment winners & losers and the objections that matter.

## What it's for (positioned honestly)

✅ **Discovery & screening** — surface concerns and objections across segments fast.
✅ **Hypothesis pre-filter** — narrow 20 ideas to the 3 worth real-money research.
✅ **Micro-segment coverage** — simulate cohorts too small to survey.
✅ **Stakeholder alignment** — a memorable, clickable artifact for steering committees.

⚠️ **Not** a replacement for real research on bet-the-business decisions — it is *directional, not predictive*, until calibrated against a client's own outcomes.

---

## The 3-minute demo

1. **Frame (15s):** "Test any decision on 1,200 synthetic customers before a dollar is spent."
2. **Audience (30s):** open a saved cohort — "high-claim auto holders, 2+ yrs tenure."
3. **Product (30s):** open the play — hypothesis, target, key lever, KPIs.
4. **Simulate (45s):** run it; watch live progress; land on Insights.
5. **Verdict (45s):** recommendation banner + headline numbers; expand one diverging segment.
6. **Wow (15s):** drop into the agent **Debate** to show the reasoning is real.

---

## The proof point that closes the deal

> **Backtest:** take one decision an EXL client already made — a real price change or campaign — run the twin *as if blind to the outcome*, and show it would have called the result. One credible backtest moves this from "interesting demo" to "buy it."
